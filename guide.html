Tombola Masterclass

How to plan, call and run a smooth tombola event from start to finish, with practical scripts, checklists and event-day structure.

Introduction

Tombola looks simple from a distance. People see the prizes, the laughter and the winner announcements. What they do not see is the structure that keeps the session from becoming messy, slow or disputed.

This guide turns tombola from a scattered oral skill into a practical event system. It is built for people who want to run a live session with confidence and, if needed, package that skill into a paid event service.

Core idea: a strong tombola session is built on preparation, fairness, rhythm and confidence.

What tombola really is

At its core, tombola is a raffle-style event segment built around chance, prizes and live audience participation. In many Nigerian settings, it is not merely a draw. It is a performance moment inside the event.

Why it still matters

  • Raises audience excitement
  • Creates participation
  • Supports fundraising
  • Makes prizes feel earned and memorable

What the caller controls

  • Pace
  • Suspense
  • Fairness perception
  • Clarity and energy in the room

The anatomy of a good session

  1. Purpose
  2. Preparation
  3. Materials
  4. Rules clarity
  5. Delivery rhythm
  6. Winner handling

If any one of these parts is weak, the whole session starts to wobble.

Planning the event properly

Prize logic

Build anticipation. Start with lighter prizes, then move toward stronger emotional peaks or the visible grand prize.

Ticket logic

Keep ticketing simple. One confusing pricing structure can cut participation before the event even warms up.

Assistant roles

Caller, draw assistant, prize assistant and verifier should each know one clear job and stay in that lane.

Stage flow

Open, explain rules, confirm participation, draw, verify, award, repeat, then close confidently.

Calling technique

The mindset

A confident caller sounds calm, not strained. You do not need to sound dramatic every second. You need to sound in control.

Voice rules

  • Speak slightly slower than casual conversation at the start
  • Repeat numbers clearly once
  • Pause enough to create anticipation, not confusion
  • Celebrate winners warmly, not mechanically

Sample opening line

Good evening everyone. We are about to begin the tombola session. Please keep your tickets close, listen carefully and stay ready as numbers are called. We will keep this orderly, lively and fair. Let us begin.

Handling common problems

No winner responds

Repeat the number once, then redraw clearly if no valid response appears.

Dispute breaks out

Pause, restate the rule, verify visibly, decide clearly, and move on without emotional argument.

Energy drops

Vary the pace. Not every round should sound equally loud or equally flat.

Helpers cause confusion

Brief assistants before the audience arrives. Public confusion usually starts backstage.
