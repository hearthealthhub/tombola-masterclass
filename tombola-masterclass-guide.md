# Tombola Masterclass

## How to plan, call and run a smooth tombola event from start to finish

## Introduction

Tombola looks simple from a distance. People see the excitement, the laughter, the prizes, the winner announcements and the social energy. What many do not see is the structure that makes the event feel smooth instead of messy.

A poor tombola session feels confused. The caller sounds uncertain. The prizes are not arranged properly. The crowd does not understand the rules clearly. Winners argue. The energy falls. What should have felt lively ends up feeling flat.

A good tombola session feels different. The flow is clear. The audience understands what is happening. The caller sounds confident. The tickets, prizes and sequence all make sense. The excitement grows naturally.

This guide turns tombola from a vague oral skill into a practical event system. It is written for people who want to plan well, call well and run a memorable session without unnecessary confusion.

This guide is not about secret knowledge. It is about structure, delivery and confidence.

## What tombola is

At its core, tombola is a raffle style event activity built around chance, prizes and live audience participation. It usually works by selling or distributing numbered tickets, drawing numbers randomly, and rewarding winners according to the event rules.

In different countries and settings, tombola has appeared in different forms. In some places it resembles bingo. In others it resembles a fairground raffle. In many Nigerian social settings, it works as a lively event segment driven by rhythm, suspense, crowd response and a confident caller.

That means tombola is not just a raffle. In practice it is a performance segment inside an event.

## Why it still matters

Tombola survives because it solves several event problems at once:

- it raises excitement
- it creates participation
- it can help raise funds
- it makes prizes feel engaging, not merely distributed
- it gives the event a memorable social peak

If run well, it becomes one of the most talked-about parts of the event.

If run badly, it becomes one of the most embarrassing parts.

## The role of the caller

The caller is not just someone who reads numbers.

The caller controls:
- pace
- suspense
- fairness perception
- audience clarity
- energy in the room
- winner confirmation
- transition between stages of the activity

That is why the skill can be monetized. A confident caller reduces confusion and increases the quality of the event experience.

## The anatomy of a good tombola event

A strong tombola session has six parts:

1. Purpose
2. Preparation
3. Materials
4. Rules clarity
5. Delivery rhythm
6. Winner handling

If any one of these is weak, the session becomes unstable.

### 1. Purpose

Before anything else, decide why the tombola exists.

Common purposes:
- fun and entertainment
- fundraising
- prize distribution with audience engagement
- social bonding at formal or semi-formal events

If purpose is unclear, everything downstream gets weak. Ticket pricing, prize quality, pace and messaging all depend on the purpose.

### 2. Preparation

Preparation includes:
- number system
- ticket control
- prize arrangement
- personnel roles
- stage flow
- sound setup
- audience instructions

### 3. Materials

At minimum, you need:
- tickets or numbered slips
- a container or draw system
- a caller script
- a winner recording method
- prize display area
- one or two assistants

### 4. Rules clarity

The audience must know:
- how tickets are obtained
- how winners are chosen
- whether one person can win more than once
- how winners are confirmed
- what happens if a winner is absent

### 5. Delivery rhythm

The session should feel alive but not rushed.

The best rhythm is controlled energy.
Not noise. Not confusion.

### 6. Winner handling

The way winners are confirmed matters.
Fairness must be visible.
If the audience does not trust the process, the mood breaks.

## Planning the event

## Step 1. Define the scale

Ask:
- how many attendees are expected
- is the audience seated, moving around, or mixed
- is the tombola a short segment or a major attraction
- are prizes symbolic, practical, premium, or sponsored

## Step 2. Decide the prize logic

Good prize logic creates anticipation.

Possible structures:
- many small prizes and one major prize
- several medium prizes and one final prize
- sponsor-backed prize sequence
- surprise prize intervals

Prizes should be visible if possible. Visible prizes create attention.

## Step 3. Set ticket logic

Decide:
- free distribution or paid sale
- one ticket per person or multiple tickets allowed
- single round or multiple rounds
- physical tickets or printed slips

If it is paid, the math must be simple. Confusing pricing reduces participation.

## Step 4. Build the team

Do not run it alone if the event is medium or large.

Recommended roles:
- caller or anchor
- draw assistant
- prize assistant
- recorder or verifier
- crowd support assistant if needed

## Step 5. Set the stage flow

A simple stage flow looks like this:

1. short introduction
2. quick explanation of rules
3. ticket confirmation
4. first draw
5. prize announcement
6. repeated rounds with escalating energy
7. final draw or grand prize
8. short closeout

## Fundraising logic

If tombola is used for fundraising, keep the message simple.

The audience should know:
- what the event segment supports
- why participation matters
- how the prizes fit into the experience

Never make the fundraising explanation too long once the session begins. Explain early, then move into action.

## Invitation logic

If the event includes tombola as a selling point, mention it clearly in invitations.

### Sample invitation wording

You are warmly invited to an evening of celebration, connection and lively moments, including a well-planned tombola session with exciting prizes.

### Shorter version

## How to call tombola confidently

## The mindset

A confident caller sounds calm, not strained.

You do not need to sound dramatic every second.
You need to sound in control.

Your job is to:
- make the rules clear
- keep the room engaged
- protect fairness
- maintain the emotional rhythm

## Voice technique

Use these rules:
- speak slightly slower than casual conversation at the beginning
- emphasize important instructions
- keep prize announcements crisp
- avoid mumbling numbers
- repeat winning numbers clearly

## Opening script

Good evening everyone. We are about to begin the tombola session. Please keep your tickets close to you, listen carefully as numbers are called, and stay alert because once your number is called and confirmed, your prize is yours. We will keep this orderly, lively and fair. Let us begin.

## Ticket sales or participation prompt

If you have not gotten your ticket yet, this is the moment to do so. Keep it safe, keep it visible, and stay ready. The first winning number will be called shortly.

## Number calling rhythm

A good caller does not just read a number. The caller frames the moment.

Example rhythm:

We are ready for the next draw.
The room is getting warm now.
Let us see who takes this one.
The number is... 27.
I repeat, 27.
If you have 27, raise your hand clearly.

Then confirm visibly.

## Prize announcement rhythm

This round is for a practical gift that somebody will be glad to take home tonight.
We are drawing now.
Your number is 42.
Four two. 42.
Please confirm your ticket clearly.

## Winner confirmation script

We have a winner. Please hold your ticket up clearly for confirmation. Thank you. Confirmed. Congratulations. Please come forward or meet our assistant for your prize.

## If no winner responds immediately

I will repeat the number once more before we move on. The number is 42. If there is no valid response now, we redraw.

## Handling disputes calmly

Never argue emotionally.

Use this pattern:
- pause
- restate the rule
- verify visibly
- decide clearly
- move on

### Dispute handling line

Let us keep it orderly. We will verify the ticket based on the rule already announced. Once verified, we continue immediately.

## Energy control

The secret is variation.

If every draw sounds equally loud, the session becomes tiring.
If every draw sounds flat, the session becomes dull.

Use waves:
- calm start
- rising middle
- stronger suspense near bigger prizes
- controlled finish

## Common mistakes

### Mistake 1. Unclear rules

Fix it by explaining the rules before the first draw.

### Mistake 2. Weak number calling

Fix it by speaking numbers clearly and repeating them once.

### Mistake 3. Poor prize sequence

Fix it by structuring prizes from lighter wins to stronger emotional peaks.

### Mistake 4. Running it alone

Fix it by using at least one assistant.

### Mistake 5. Talking too much

Fix it by being concise between draws.

### Mistake 6. Sounding uncertain

Fix it by rehearsing opening, verification and winner lines.

## Tombola planning checklist

### Pre-event checklist

- define event purpose
- estimate attendance
- decide ticket system
- decide prize structure
- assign assistants
- prepare scripts
- prepare winner sheet
- prepare materials
- test sound
- review rules once before start

### Prize sourcing checklist

- list available prizes
- label each prize
- confirm prize sequence
- set final grand prize if any
- arrange display neatly

### Event day checklist

- arrive early
- confirm microphone works
- brief assistants
- confirm draw method
- keep tickets ready
- state rules clearly
- begin on time
- record winners immediately
- manage tempo carefully
- close confidently

### Post-event review checklist

- what worked
- what caused delay
- which prizes created the strongest response
- whether rules were clear enough
- how to improve next time
